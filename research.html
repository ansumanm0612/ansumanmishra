research interests

My primary interests are in formal semantics and pragmatics, especially the semantics of modality, negation, and information structure in South Asian languages. I am particularly drawn to questions about how causal dependencies shape modal meaning, and how presuppositional and actuality inferences interact with negation in Hindi-Urdu. I also work on Odia, where I have looked at definiteness marking, motion event typology, and focus particles.

A secondary thread runs through computational and corpus-based approaches to Indian language resources — morphological disambiguation, word sense disambiguation, and lexicographic development.

thesis

  • Mishra, A. (in progress). Causal Architecture, Actuality & Negation in Hindi-Urdu Modality. MA thesis, The English and Foreign Languages University, Hyderabad.
    Analyses the sak-/pā- modal distinction using causal premise semantics, event-relativity, and two-dimensional projection. Argues for a split between a CAUSE-quantifier (pā-) and a PERMIT-quantifier (sak-), with presuppositional actuality entailments derived from causal structure.
