I am a Master's student in Computational Linguistics at the English and Foreign Languages University (EFLU), Hyderabad. My research is at the intersection of formal semantics, pragmatics, and South Asian languages, with a focus on the syntax-semantics-pragmatics interface.

My current work centres on modality and negation in Hindi-Urdu. My MA thesis, Causal Architecture, Actuality & Negation in Hindi-Urdu Modality, analyses the sak-/pā- modal distinction using causal premise semantics, event-relativity, and two-dimensional projection. More broadly, I am interested in how meaning shifts with context — because people rarely say exactly what they mean.

I also work on Odia, where I have looked at definiteness marking, motion event typology, and focus particles. Before EFLU, I studied Linguistics at the University of Hyderabad. I grew up in Odisha but have called Hyderabad home for most of my life.

When I am not doing linguistics, I watch cricket or chess, write poetry, read philosophy, or fall into rabbit holes of facts with zero real-world relevance.
