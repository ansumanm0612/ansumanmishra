about me

I am a Master's student in Computational Linguistics at The English and Foreign Languages University (EFLU), Hyderabad. My research sits at the intersection of formal semantics, pragmatics, and South Asian languages, with a focus on the syntax-semantics-pragmatics interface.

My MA thesis, Causal Architecture, Actuality & Negation in Hindi-Urdu Modality, analyses the sak-/pā- modal distinction through causal premise semantics, event-relativity, and two-dimensional projection. More broadly, I am interested in how meaning changes as a function of context — because humans rarely say exactly what they mean.

I work primarily on Odia, Hindi-Urdu, and Telugu. Before EFLU, I studied Linguistics at the University of Hyderabad, where I began with syntax and slowly migrated south toward semantics and pragmatics. I grew up in Odisha but have called Hyderabad home for most of my life.

When I am not doing linguistics, I watch cricket or chess, write poetry, read philosophy, or fall down rabbit holes of facts with zero real-world relevance.
